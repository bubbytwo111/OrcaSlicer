# ROCK3TS 3D Beginner Guide

This guide is for first-time users who want a simple, safe starting point.

## 1. First Launch

When you open ROCK3TS 3D for the first time:

- Let the app finish loading fully.
- If a setup wizard appears, choose your printer only if you are sure it matches your real machine.
- If you are not sure, stop there and confirm your printer model, bed size, firmware type, and nozzle size before printing.

Important:

- Do not guess printer settings.
- Wrong printer settings can cause failed prints or machine crashes.

## 2. Main Areas Of The App

The app is built around a few main parts:

- The center area shows your model on the print bed.
- The left side is usually where objects and plates are managed.
- The right side is where print, filament, and printer settings live.
- The top toolbar is where you open files, save projects, slice, and export.

## 3. Before You Print Anything

Always check these 4 things:

- The selected printer matches your real printer.
- The selected nozzle size matches your printer.
- The selected filament matches what is loaded.
- The bed shape and print volume look correct.

If any of those are wrong, do not print yet.

## 4. Open A Model

To open a model:

1. Click `Open` or drag your `.stl`, `.3mf`, or `.step` file into the app.
2. Wait for the model to appear on the bed.
3. Make sure it is not floating, partly outside the bed, or way too large.

If needed:

- Use `Arrange` to place parts automatically.
- Use move, rotate, or scale tools carefully.

## 5. Pick Settings The Safe Way

If you are a beginner, change as little as possible at first.

Start by checking:

- `Printer`
- `Filament`
- `Process` or `Print Settings`

Good beginner rule:

- use known printer presets
- use known filament presets
- avoid changing advanced speed or temperature settings until you understand them

## 6. Slice The Model

When everything looks right:

1. Click `Slice`.
2. Wait for the preview to generate.
3. Look through the layers before exporting.

Check for:

- missing parts
- strange gaps
- supports where you expect them
- print time that seems reasonable

## 7. Save The Print File

If the preview looks good:

1. Click `Export G-code` or `Save`.
2. Save the file somewhere easy to find.
3. Put that file on your printer the normal way for your machine.

## 8. First Print Safety Check

Before starting a real print:

- Make sure the bed is clear.
- Make sure the correct filament is loaded.
- Make sure the nozzle is clean.
- Stay near the printer for the first layer.

Stop the print if:

- the nozzle drags into the bed
- filament does not stick
- the printer moves outside normal limits
- the print starts in the wrong place

## 9. What Beginners Should Avoid

Until you are comfortable, avoid changing:

- start G-code
- end G-code
- machine limits
- acceleration
- pressure advance
- custom firmware settings
- bed size
- nozzle diameter

Those settings can change printer behavior in unsafe ways.

## 10. Best Beginner Workflow

Use this order every time:

1. Open model
2. Confirm printer
3. Confirm filament
4. Confirm nozzle
5. Slice
6. Check layer preview
7. Export G-code
8. Watch first layer

## 11. If Something Looks Wrong

If a print preview looks wrong:

- do not export yet
- go back and re-check printer and filament presets
- look at model size and placement
- check support settings

If the printer itself behaves strangely:

- stop the print
- do not keep testing random settings
- go back to known-good presets

## 12. Good First Projects

Best beginner test models:

- a small calibration cube
- a simple flat tag
- a small storage hook
- a low-risk benchy if your printer is already working well

Avoid very large or complicated prints for your first test.

## 13. Final Tip

The safest way to learn is:

- change one thing at a time
- test small parts first
- keep notes about what worked
